## Content Details
Strictly focused on Vocabulary: Affect vs Effect Distinction. No content overlap with other sections.

## Formulas/Patterns
- **The RAVEN Mnemonic**: 
  - **R**emember
  - **A**ffect = **V**erb
  - **E**ffect = **N**oun
- **Common Collocations (Effect - Noun)**:
  - *Have an effect on*: The new policy had a positive effect on the economy.
  - *Take effect*: The law will take effect starting next month.
  - *Side effects*: Some medications may cause mild side effects like fatigue.
- **Common Collocations (Affect - Verb)**:
  - *Directly affect*: Global warming directly affects biodiversity in the Arctic.
  - *Adversely affect*: The rainy weather adversely affected the crop yields.

## Content Details
- **Effect** (danh từ): dùng khi nêu ảnh hưởng đến = nói về kết quả của sự ảnh hưởng.
  - *Example*: Playing too many games will have a bad effect on your eyes.
- **Affect** (động từ): dùng khi hành động gây ảnh hưởng.
  - *Example*: Stress affects people’s health.

## Examples
- **Nouns (Effect)**:
  - "I ate a lot of McDonald's that led to a negative effect on my health."
  - "The soothing music had a calming effect on the crying baby."
- **Verbs (Affect)**:
  - "Climate change is affecting the migration patterns of birds."
  - "The test results will affect your final grade for the semester."

## Advanced Word Forms
- **Effective (Adj)**: Produce the intended result (e.g., An effective strategy).
- **Effectively (Adv)**: In a way that produces results (e.g., She communicates effectively).
- **Effectiveness (Noun)**: The degree to which something is successful.

## Common Mistakes
- **Incorrect**: "The noise did not affect on me." -> **Correct**: "The noise did not affect me." (Affect is a transitive verb, no preposition needed).
- **Incorrect**: "The rain had a bad affect on our plans." -> **Correct**: "The rain had a bad effect on our plans."
- **Nuance**: In rare academic cases, "effect" can be a verb meaning *to bring about* (e.g., to effect change), and "affect" can be a noun meaning *emotional state*. However, in IELTS, sticking to the RAVEN rule is recommended.
